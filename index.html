About Me

Two skill sets,
one operator.

I run paid media the same way performance teams build product — data-first, evidence-based, every decision testable. No "creative intuition," no "trust me, this'll work." Audience research, structured creative testing, dynamic budget allocation against live CPA.

I started in 2017, built my own social presence, published case studies, and took on first clients through word of mouth. Over 8 years the work scaled: from local businesses in Eastern Europe to $3M/month across 7 platforms in the USA.

The last 4–5 years I've focused heavily on creative strategy — because that's where the real leverage shifted. As Meta, TikTok, and Google moved targeting control to their algorithms, the creative became the targeting. The hook, the message, the format now determine who sees your ad. Most media buyers haven't caught up to this yet.

I don't make promises before I understand your business. If paid media can move the needle for you — I'll tell you exactly how. If it can't — I'll tell you that too.

FAQ

Frequently
asked questions.

What type of businesses do you work with?
+
I work primarily with DTC e-commerce brands, lead generation businesses, online education, and high-ticket services. Verticals include health & wellness, telecom, SaaS, immigration, travel & lifestyle goods, and luxury / high-ticket products. The common thread: brands with serious ad budgets ($20K+/month) that want a real system, not just someone to "run ads."
What ad spend do I need to work with you?
+
For monthly retainers, the recommended minimum is $20K+/month in ad spend — below that, the math rarely works for either of us. For audits ($299–$350), there's no minimum — these are useful for any brand running ads, regardless of budget size.
Do I need to commit to a long-term contract?
+
Retainers are month-to-month with 30-day notice. No 3-month or 6-month lock-ins. That said, paid media isn't a 30-day game — meaningful scaling happens in months 2–4. Most clients stay 6+ months because the system compounds. The flexibility is there if it's not working — but in my experience, when expectations are aligned upfront, it's rarely needed.
How long until I see results?
+
Honest answer: it depends on where you start. If you have an existing account with stable creatives, I can usually move CPA in 2–4 weeks. If we're launching from zero — building creative pipelines, testing frameworks, audience research — meaningful results take 6–8 weeks. Anyone promising overnight results is either lying or working with very low standards for "results."
Do I need to provide creatives or do you make them?
+
Both options exist. The Media Buying retainer assumes you provide creatives. The MB + Creative Strategy retainer includes the full creative pipeline — concepts, briefs, and coordination with designers/editors/UGC creators (your team or mine). Most clients find the combined option drives meaningfully better results because the creative is built specifically to perform in the ad environment.
What makes you different from agencies or other freelancers?
+
Most media buyers don't build creatives, and most creative strategists don't buy media. I do both — which means the creative strategy is built specifically to perform in the platform environment I'm managing. No handoff delays, no context loss, no finger-pointing when results aren't there. One system, one person accountable.
How do you measure success?
+
Primary KPIs are agreed upfront: CPA, ROAS, or CPL depending on the business model. I track intermediate metrics (CPC, CTR, hook rate, scroll-stop rate) to diagnose creative performance. Winning creatives must also be scalable — I don't call something a winner if it only works at $200/day.
Do you sign NDAs and protect client data?
+
Yes — happy to sign NDAs before we share any sensitive numbers or strategy details. All client work is treated as confidential by default. Case studies on this site reference specific results but do not name brands without written permission.
What happens after the audit — am I obligated to continue?
+
Zero obligation. The audit is a standalone deliverable — you get a document with specific action points and you can implement them yourself, with your existing team, or with another freelancer. Most clients who do continue do it because the audit demonstrated where the gaps are. No retainer pressure either way.
Can you take on new clients right now?
+
I take on a limited number of retainer clients to maintain quality. Book a discovery call to discuss current availability and timelines. Audits and one-time projects are generally available within 1–2 weeks.
